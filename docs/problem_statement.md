# Problem Statement

Pearl farming requires precise monitoring of water quality parameters such as temperature, pH, and turbidity. Traditional methods rely heavily on manual observation, which is time-consuming and prone to human error.

Inconsistent monitoring can lead to poor water conditions, affecting oyster health and ultimately reducing pearl quality and yield.

This project aims to develop an IoT-based system that continuously monitors and regulates environmental conditions in pearl farming, ensuring optimal growth conditions and improving productivity.

---

# Use Case

- Automated monitoring of water quality  
- Real-time alerts for abnormal conditions  
- Reduced manual effort  
- Improved pearl production efficiency
